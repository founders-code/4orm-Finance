Inside the Canadian regulatory perimeter. By design.

4orm Finance is built to operate inside Canada's existing regulatory framework, not around it. OSFI, CSA, FINTRAC, CIRO. Custody under the CIRO Digital Asset Custody Framework. The four regulators

One rail. Four supervisory feeds.

Every transaction on 4orm Finance produces evidence for four distinct regulatory surfaces. Each one is named, each one has a specific supervisory expectation, each one receives a tailored feed at the moment of commit.

OSFI

Capital & liquidity

Federally regulated banks and trust companies operate under OSFI capital and liquidity rules. 4orm Finance produces the reports OSFI needs to supervise the institutions on the rail.

Capital rules
CSA

Securities & disclosure

Canadian Securities Administrators set the continuous-disclosure and marketplace rules for tokenized securities. Project Tokenization is the national initiative. 4orm Finance operates inside it.

National coordination
CIRO

Custody & marketplace

CIRO's Digital Asset Custody Framework (February 2026) is the reference standard for institutional digital custody in Canada. 4orm Trust Co targets the Tier-2 pathway. 4ormEx operates under CIRO marketplace oversight.

Custody framework
FINTRAC

AML / ATF

FINTRAC LCTR and EFT thresholds, suspicious transaction reporting, KYC and beneficial ownership rules. AML rule engines run pre-trade at the rail. Reporting is emitted from the ledger.

AML at the rail

Inside the perimeter, by design

Compliance is not a cost center. It is the product.

Every other tokenization architecture treats compliance as overhead bolted on top of the rail. 4orm Finance inverts the model. The compliance surface is the rail. Pre-trade screens block prohibited transactions. Post-trade attestation runs in the same block as settlement. Reporting is what falls out of the ledger.

Pre-trade

Screens at the order book

Sanctions, PEP, beneficial-ownership screens run before a transaction enters the order book. Prohibited transactions never become trades.

Block on entry
In-trade

Attested at commit

Post-trade attestation runs in the same block as settlement. The attestation is a feature of the trade, not a separate operation.

Inline attest
Post-trade

Reporting from the ledger

OSFI, CSA, FINTRAC, CIRO reporting are generated from the ledger as the rail clears. There is no reconciliation. There is no batch.
