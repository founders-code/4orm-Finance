The system

The whole picture, three flows.

An internal transfer that never leaves your bank, an interbank settlement through the 4orm rail, and the legacy correspondent route that 4orm replaces. Each gets its own diagram, so the architecture isn't abstract.

What you are seeing. An on-us transfer is one bank moving its own money on its own ledger. Inside the blue Bank A perimeter on the left: customer accounts, the core banking system, the journal entry (debit one account, credit the other), and the single-bank compliance gate. The faded grey panels on the right show what the 4orm rail and external regulators would do if they were involved, for on-us flows they are not. Your core handles this today, and will continue to.

Even on a single-bank transfer · 4orm can still add value

The settlement stays on your core. The surrounding services can still come from the shared rail.

4orm is not a second balance sheet for your on-us transfer. We never touch the journal entry, the customer accounts, or your core's record of the transaction. But your bank can opt into the rail's compliance, KYC, audit, and supervisory services as overlays, without ever putting the on-us flow on the rail. Five things become available:

1
Reusable KYC credential

If the customer is already KYC'd at your bank, the 4orm reuse hub can issue an institution-attested credential. The next time the same customer is onboarded on another product or with a partner, the credential can be presented under PCMLTFA s.105 reliance. You remain the issuer of record.

2
Consistent compliance screening

The same canonical sanctions list and screening logic the rail uses can run as an overlay against your on-us flows, so internal and external screening stays consistent across the same customer, the same day, the same list version.

3
Optional canonical attestation

For high-value or audit-sensitive on-us transfers, write a hash-chained attestation of the transaction to the canonical registry as an additional witness. Your core's audit log remains primary; the canonical attestation is an independent corroboration.

4
Aggregate supervisory visibility

Aggregate metrics (volume, AML alert rates, exception counts) can feed into the same read-only supervisory dashboard your interbank flows feed. Supervisors see one coherent picture of your bank's activity, on-us included, without ever reading core banking detail.

5
Cross-product risk view

Your risk team can see how a single customer's on-us activity sits next to their interbank rail activity in one customer-360 view. The data never leaves your perimeter; the view is composed on demand for your CRO, CAMLO, and CFO.

Where the line is. 4orm never touches your journal entry, your customer accounts, or your core's record of the transaction. Settlement of an on-us transfer remains entirely on your balance sheet, today and after you join the rail. 4orm's role on on-us flows is operational efficiency and audit / compliance overlay, opt-in per service, never settlement substitution.

The settled message returns to Bank B over the same API. Customer B is credited at the exact same moment Customer A is debited. No T+2 exposure window.

Frequently asked

The questions Canadian institutions, public-sector treasurers, and tokenization issuers ask before joining a closed-loop pilot.

Answers — plain English.

Is 4orm Finance a crypto exchange? +

No. 4orm Finance is institution-grade infrastructure for real-world assets — tokenized bonds, tokenized deposits, real estate, private credit, resource and infrastructure assets. It does not list speculative crypto-assets, does not serve retail traders, and does not custody bearer assets outside of a regulated perimeter.

How is 4orm Finance different from KCS Capital? +

KCS Capital is the independent technology and research firm that develops the platform. 4orm Finance operates it as a separately incorporated, independently governed regulated entity — structured as HoldCo / OpCo / CustodyCo per the CIRO Digital Asset Custody Framework.

What's the regulatory pathway? +

4orm is engaging with CIRO (marketplace and custody), CSA (Project Tokenization), OSFI (banking-aligned settlement), and FINTRAC (AML/ATF) on a coordinated pathway. The sandbox application is targeted for Q3 2026. The custody entity is structured against the CIRO Digital Asset Custody Framework Tier-2 target from day one.

Do banks have to run a blockchain to plug in? +

No. The blockchain is the execution surface underneath; the regulated control plane stays with the institution. Banks integrate via standard rails — ISO 20022, APIs, and existing core banking and treasury systems. The deployment model is a closed-loop pilot first, never a big-bang migration.

Who can hold tokens on 4orm? +

Verified, whitelisted institutional members only. Transfer rules are embedded in the token contracts; no bearer transfers, no anonymous wallets. KYC/AML credentials are reusable across the network so onboarding compounds rather than repeats.

What is the settlement asset? +

4orm settles in tokenized CAD. The cash leg is either a regulated Canadian-dollar stablecoin (e.g., Stablecorp's QCAD) or bank-issued tokenized CAD deposits — depending on the participating institution's preference and regulatory posture.

What does the partnership model look like for tokenization projects? +

Issuers like exempt market dealers, real estate platforms, mining and energy projects, and private credit funds keep their dealer registration and asset specialization. 4orm provides the institutional bridge: bank counterparties, secondary liquidity, an institutional investor pool, and the secondary market needed to actually offload at scale.

How can a municipality or government engage? +

Through a discovery engagement and, when appropriate, a closed-loop pilot. Use cases include tokenized municipal debt, infrastructure revenue financing, sovereign and sub-sovereign instruments, and Crown-aligned tokenized programs that benefit from a Canadian-sovereign rail.

When does the platform go live? +

4orm Finance is in pre-seed, closing Q2 2026. Closed-loop pilots are opening throughout 2026. The architecture, custody framework, and regulatory pathway are in flight today. Production timing depends on regulatory sandbox progression and pilot results.
