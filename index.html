The architectural philosophy

The regulated operational core stays inside the platform. Everything else, chains, custodians, KYC providers, interoperability frameworks, sits in bounded integration domains, all swappable behind a single abstraction. This is how the rail stays neutral and stays regulated at the same time.

What we own. What we integrate.

The platform never yields control of the ledger of record, the compliance engine, or the settlement logic. Everything else is an adapter. That posture is how 4orm stays provider-neutral and regulator-aligned at the same time.

The end-to-end lifecycle

One regulated rail covers every state of a tokenized real-world asset, from EMD-governed primary issuance through atomic settlement, qualified custody, regulatory reporting, and final redemption. The same controls apply to every step.

One platform. The full lifecycle.

Every state of a tokenized RWA, issuance, trading, settlement, custody, reporting, redemption, happens inside one regulated rail. No drop-offs to external bridges, no handoffs to unsupervised ledgers, no off-platform exception books.

The 4orm settlement principle
Pillar 03 in focus · Settlement

The whole picture, three flows.

An internal transfer that never leaves your bank, an interbank settlement through the 4orm rail, and the legacy correspondent route that 4orm replaces. Each gets its own diagram, so the architecture isn't abstract.

What you are seeing. An on-us transfer is one bank moving its own money on its own ledger. Inside the blue Bank A perimeter on the left: customer accounts, the core banking system, the journal entry (debit one account, credit the other), and the single-bank compliance gate. The faded grey panels on the right show what the 4orm rail and external regulators would do if they were involved, for on-us flows they are not. Your core handles this today, and will continue to.

Even on a single-bank transfer · 4orm can still add value

The settlement stays on your core. The surrounding services can still come from the shared rail.

4orm is not a second balance sheet for your on-us transfer. We never touch the journal entry, the customer accounts, or your core's record of the transaction. But your bank can opt into the rail's compliance, KYC, audit, and supervisory services as overlays, without ever putting the on-us flow on the rail. Five things become available:

1
Reusable KYC credential

If the customer is already KYC'd at your bank, the 4orm reuse hub can issue an institution-attested credential. The next time the same customer is onboarded on another product or with a partner, the credential can be presented under PCMLTFA s.105 reliance. You remain the issuer of record.

2
Consistent compliance screening

The same canonical sanctions list and screening logic the rail uses can run as an overlay against your on-us flows, so internal and external screening stays consistent across the same customer, the same day, the same list version.

3
Optional canonical attestation

For high-value or audit-sensitive on-us transfers, write a hash-chained attestation of the transaction to the canonical registry as an additional witness. Your core's audit log remains primary; the canonical attestation is an independent corroboration.

4
Aggregate supervisory visibility

Aggregate metrics (volume, AML alert rates, exception counts) can feed into the same read-only supervisory dashboard your interbank flows feed. Supervisors see one coherent picture of your bank's activity, on-us included, without ever reading core banking detail.

5
Cross-product risk view

Your risk team can see how a single customer's on-us activity sits next to their interbank rail activity in one customer-360 view. The data never leaves your perimeter; the view is composed on demand for your CRO, CAMLO, and CFO.

Where the line is. 4orm never touches your journal entry, your customer accounts, or your core's record of the transaction. Settlement of an on-us transfer remains entirely on your balance sheet, today and after you join the rail. 4orm's role on on-us flows is operational efficiency and audit / compliance overlay, opt-in per service, never settlement substitution.

Interoperability by design

Every integration boundary is an adapter, not a hard dependency. That means the rail is portable across cash assets, custodians, ledgers, KYC providers, and the institution's existing core banking systems.

How it plugs into your institution.

Cash adapter

Tokenized CAD, your choice

Bank-issued tokenized deposit OR regulated CAD stablecoin. Adapter abstracts the cash-leg provider so the institution picks the asset that fits its posture.

Stablecorp QCAD · bank-issued tokenized deposits · others on integration
Ledger + chain adapters

Multi-chain by design

Canonical write to the 4orm ledger. Execution-surface portability via swappable chain adapters. The platform never yields control of the ledger of record to a chain or a bridge.

Phase 1: Ethereum · Stellar · Phase 3: omni-chain via LayerZero · CCIP · Wormhole · IBC (all swappable)
Institution adapter

ISO 20022 / API / SFTP

Connects to your existing core banking, treasury, and payments systems. No core replacement. The control plane sits beside, not on top of, what you already run.

ISO 20022 messaging · REST API · SFTP batch · webhook listeners
Custody adapter

Qualified custodian, your choice

Use a CIRO-qualified Canadian trust company, an in-house HSM, or a multi-custodian split. The adapter handles confirmation, attestation, and recovery.

Tetra Trust · in-house HSM · multi-custodian splits · others on integration
KYC adapter

PCMLTFA s.105 reliance

Re-use existing institution-attested KYC credentials across the network under a written reliance framework. Each FI revalidates to its own risk policy.

Institution-attested KYC · reusable verifiable credentials · per-FI risk overlay
Supervisory adapter

Read-only feeds per regulator

OSFI, CSA, FINTRAC, BoC, FSRA, and AMF each get an aggregate, privacy-preserving read-only feed. Never reads core banking. STRs filed by the institution.

Aggregate metrics · privacy-preserving aggregation · institution-led filing
