The 4orm Finance Platform

Six capabilities. One regulated rail.

4orm Finance is full-stack institutional infrastructure for Canada. Not a crypto exchange. Six embedded capabilities, each engineered to one outcome: settlement is final at commit, every time, for every asset class on the rail.

01 · Issuance

Tokenized asset issuance

Native infrastructure for tokenizing real-world assets. Smart contracts, on-chain registry, legal-economic binding, full audit trail. Bonds, deposits, real estate, private credit, energy royalties, precious metals.

02 · Trading

4ormEx, the institutional venue

Pillar 02 operates as 4ormEx, the regulated trading layer. Order book, RFQ, secondary liquidity, multi-bank settlement corridors, all under CIRO marketplace oversight. No bridges, no multi-day windows.

24/7 secondary liquidity
03 · Settlement

Atomic settlement, final at commit

T+0 institutional settlement. Cash leg, token leg, custody, and ledger commit atomically, or none. Replaces multi-day deferred-net cycles. Frees trapped capital. Reduces counterparty risk to zero at the moment of commit.

T+0 atomic DvP
04 · Custody

Trust Co, regulated digital custody

4orm Trust Co operates as the CIRO Tier-2 qualified digital custodian. Segregated reserves, attested 1:1, supervised under the CIRO Digital Asset Custody Framework. The custody layer banks already trust.

CIRO Tier 2 pathway
05 · Reporting

Regulator-aligned reporting

OSFI, CSA, FINTRAC, and CIRO reporting embedded at the rail. No bolt-on. No post-hoc reconciliation. Reporting is what falls out of the ledger, not what your team builds after the fact.

Embedded reporting

Embedded supervisory controls

Real-time supervisory feeds for the regulators that approve the rail. Pre-trade screens, post-trade attestation, AML rule engines, KYC reusable credentials. Supervision lives with the platform.

The validation moment

The Bank of Canada has answered the question. The remaining question is who builds it.

On March 6, 2026, the Bank of Canada completed Project Samara, Canada's first tokenized bond trial, with RBC, TD, and Export Development Canada. C$100M in tokenized bonds. Full lifecycle: issuance, bidding, coupon payments, secondary trading, redemption. Regulatory sign-off from the OSC, AMF, and CIRO. The question of whether Canada will operate on tokenized rails has been answered.
