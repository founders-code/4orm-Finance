The Platform · Six capabilities, one rail

Six embedded capabilities. One regulated rail.

4orm Finance is full-stack institutional infrastructure for Canadian capital markets. Every capability below is a pillar of the same rail: issuance, trading, settlement, custody, reporting, and supervision. They share one identity layer, one ledger, one supervisory feed, one regulator-aligned reporting surface. The whole is what makes settlement final at commit.

Pillar 03 · Settlement

Atomic settlement. Final at commit.

The defining capability. Cash leg, token leg, custody, and ledger commit atomically, or none of them commit. Settlement is final when the block is final. Replaces multi-day deferred-net cycles with a single supervised transaction. The Bank of Canada validated this architecture through Project Samara.

Cash leg

Tokenized CAD

Bank-issued tokenized CAD deposits sit alongside the asset on the same rail. Not retail stablecoins. Bank deposits issued by regulated FIs, denominated in CAD.

Bank-issued
Token leg

Asset commit

The asset's on-chain representation commits in the same block as the cash leg. Issuance, transfer, secondary trade all use the same atomicity guarantee.

Atomic DvP
Custody + ledger

All-or-none commit

Custody record, regulatory record, and ledger record all commit in the same atomic operation. There is no window in which they can be out of sync.

Final at commit
Pillar 04 · Custody · 4orm Trust Co

Regulated digital custody. Tier 2 CIRO.

4orm Trust Co is the qualified digital custodian inside the 4orm family. Pathway target: CIRO Digital Asset Custody Framework, Tier 2. Segregated reserves. Attested 1:1. Supervised under the same framework that custodies traditional securities. The custody layer banks already trust.

Segregation

Customer assets segregated

Customer assets are segregated from operational reserves, attested by an independent qualifier, and reconcilable in real time against the ledger.

Independent attestation
CIRO Tier 2

Regulator-aligned custody

Pathway target is CIRO Tier 2. The framework was published in February 2026 and forms the reference standard for institutional digital custody in Canada.

Pathway target

1:1 attested, continuously

Pillars 05 + 06 · Reporting and Supervision

Reporting is what falls out of the ledger, not what your team builds after the fact.

OSFI, CSA, FINTRAC, and CIRO reporting are embedded at the rail. Supervision feeds are real-time. Pre-trade screens, post-trade attestation, AML rule engines, KYC reusable credentials. The supervisory state of every transaction is visible to the regulators that approve the rail, at the moment of commit.

Pillar 05 · Reporting
